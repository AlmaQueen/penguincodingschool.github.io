Introduction:

This website captures the importance of Porpoises and their unique abilities. It explores the endangerment of them and how to put a stop to it.

Save The Porpoises!

There is a huge issue involving the Vaquita Porpoises that not very many people know about. The issue is so bad that they could be extinct in four years! They are being killed by getting caught in illegal nets called gill nets. They're only about 30 Vaquita's left.Not many people are doing anything to stop this. That's the main reason I made this website, to support the saving of the Vaquita Porpoises. But sadly, they are not the only endangered Porpoise. The Finless Porpoise and a few others are also endangred but not as severly.

Vaquita

The Vaquita Porpoises are critically endangered. There are only about 30 left in the wild. Fishermen (and women) that fish using gill nets sometimes catch porpoises who are trying to simply catch themselves a dinner and get the porpoises killed due to them drowning in the nets. A few years ago there were 60 Vaquitas left in the wild but the poputation has dropped dirasticly. The Vaquita is the most endangered porpoise out of them all yet sadly, one of the cutest. I hope you learned something about the Vaquita Porpose!

Fun Fact

The name Vaquita means Little Cow.

Harbor

The Harbor Porpoise (aka the common porpoise) is probably the most common species of porpoise. They live in the temprate costal waters of the Northern Hemisphere. Once they were considereda delacacy upon royals. I'm just glad it was a delacacy other than a common meat, if so, every species of Porpoise would probably be extinct!

Finless

The Finless Porpoise (hence the name) dosen't have a dorsal fin, making it the only species of porpoise that lacks this. The Finless Porpoise is the most rooted member of the porpoise family.
